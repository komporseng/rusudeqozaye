Biography

Tom Sachs was born in New York City, NY on July 26, 1966. American sculptor and contemporary artist who is best known for having recreated several modern icons using brand packaging and other everyday materials. One of his most famous pieces was a recreation of Le Corbusier‘s 1952 Unité d’Habitation. In 2000 he organized an art show titled American Bricolage that featured work from Alexander Calder. He spent two years working in Frank Gehry‘s Los Angeles based furniture shop.
