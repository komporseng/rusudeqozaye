What Time Will RuPauls Drag Race Down Under Episode 2 Premiere?

July 2024 · 4 minute read

RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under showed off a whole lot of Perth-onality in its debut episode. We met 10 drag queens from Australia and New Zealand and saw 30 looks. Drag Race is a franchise that knows how to make every single minute of its runtime count.

So, what’s next for these Aussie and Kiwi queens? Uh, Snatch Game, that’s what! The twists and turns keep a’coming! So how can you watch RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under if you live here in the States? And what time does Episode 2 premiere? We’ve got all the info you need!

Where to watch Drag Race Down Under? How to watch Drag Race Down Under in the US

If you live in the United States (and really anywhere in the world other than the UK, Canada, and Down Under), you can stream RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under via the streaming service WOW Presents Plus. If you’re not yet a subscriber, you can sign up for a free, 7-day trial of WOW Presents Plus. The service is $3.99 a month after that.

How to watch Drag Race Down Under in Australia and New Zealand

For everyone who lives in Australia and New Zealand, you’ll be able to stream your hometown queens on services you may already have. Drag Race Down Under will stream on Stan in Australia, and it will air on NZTV in New Zealand.

How to watch Drag Race Down Under in the UK and Canada

If you live in the UK, you won’t find RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under on Netflix. Instead, it’s streaming on BBC Three’s iPlayer—just like RuPaul’s Drag Race UK.  As for Canada, you can stream RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under on Crave.

What time does RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under Episode 2 premiere on WOW Presents Plus in the United States?

The second episode of RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under will debut in the US on WOW Presents Plus on Saturday, May 8 at 3 a.m. ET / 12 p.m. PT. Wake up, pour some coffee, and get sickening. As for the rest of the world, here’s when you’ll be able to stream RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under on Saturdays:

  • New Zealand at 6 p.m. NZST on TVNZ
  • Australia at 4 p.m. AEST on Stan
  • The UK at 9 a.m. BST on BBC iPlayer
  • America at 3 a.m. ET on WOW Presents Plus

If you want to know more about the RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under premiere, we’ve got a recap for you below.

What happened on RPDR Down Under Episode 1 ?

The queens entered the new Werk Room with plenty of drama. Not only did the queens question Elektra Shock’s lewk and wig, they also could not get over the fact that she’s competing against both of her bosses. And those bosses? Y’all, Kita Mean and Anita Wigl’it have a simmering rivalry going too. But Elektra showed the competition what she was capable of by winning the screen test mini challenge (with guest appearance from Taika Waititi).

On the main stage, categories were Born Naked and No Place Like Home. Art Simone stunned in a sparkling sci-fi nude illusion and Melbourne-inspired graffiti cloak look; Scarlet Adams took “born naked” further than anyone in a full body, anatomically correct latex nude suit and then served glamour in a black swan gown; and Karen from Finance took home the win with her campy AF day at the beach glitter bodysuit and fashion-forward day at the races chevron look.

Elektra Shock and Jojo Zaho landed in the bottom, Elektra for a confusing hometown look and Jojo for not paying attention to the details in either of her looks. The two dueled to “Tragedy” by the Bee Gees and Elektra turned it out. Spins and splits—we may have a lip sync assassin on our hands. And her first victim was Jojo Zaho, who sashayed away.
